In Finem Imperii you can create up to 3 characters in fictional open worlds. Each character will start in a realm of your choice.

Turns and time

Your character will start with 15 days available to spend in whichever actions you choose. Some actions will cost little or no time, while other may cost most of the time your character has.

Each day at 8:00 AM and 8:00 PM Central European Time (or summer time) a turn will pass for all characters. This will regenerate the amount of time your character can use.

Some actions will only take effect when a turn passes, for example, when you travel to a different region.

On cooperation

Trying to go your own way without cooperating with others is definitely the hard way to play this game. You are much more likely to be successful and have a larger impact in the world by playing together with others, especially if you are new to the game.

The most common way to play is by taking an active part in a realm and stay faithful to it, obeying superiors and eventually gaining more influence or even reaching some position of responsibility. Maybe you can even get to lead a realm! But, of course, it's entirely up to you to choose how you want to play your character.

The main form of communication is via text messages.

Power relationships

Relationships of power of some characters over others is a key aspect of this game. You should try to be always aware of those relationships, especially those affecting your character directly. You want to avoid faux pas with those who have direct influence on your own character.

But being aware of power relationships among your rivals can also prove useful. Disabling the leader of an enemy realm is always very useful on your way to victory.

You vs. your character

Finem Imperii is a role-playing game. That means that you should play the role of your character and understand that your chacater and you are different people. Much like Johnny Depp and Captain Jack Sparrow: Johnny plays the character, but he is not really a pirate. He is just playing a role.

In many games you find yourself doing things you would never do in real life. In this game you should find yourself also talking in ways you would not do in real life. You want to communicate appropiately according to the situation. For example, if you are just a newcomer to a realm and write a letter to your King, you may want to be courteous and humble. You don't want to anger your King, do you?
